Why Corporate Agile Is Just Theater

Podcast

About this episode. In many large organizations, “being Agile” has become a performance: standups, retros, and sprint boards are in place, but delivery stays slow, decisions still flow through layers of approval, and teams feel more constrained than empowered. The podcast argues that in those settings, Agile is often theater—ritual without the conditions that make Agile effective.

The discussion connects to the themes in this SDLC collection: why Agile works best for small, well-bounded teams; how communication overhead grows with team size; and why success depends on aligning your process with your actual structure (and sometimes choosing Waterfall, SAFe, or hybrid models) instead of defaulting to Agile everywhere.
