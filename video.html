The Agile Paradox

Video

About this video. Agile promises faster delivery, better collaboration, and continuous improvement—yet many teams that “go Agile” end up with more ceremonies, more overhead, and the same old delivery problems. That tension is the Agile Paradox: the very practices meant to simplify work can, when misapplied or scaled without care, make it more complex.

This video explores why that happens and what it means for how we choose and run our development life cycles. It ties directly to the ideas in this collection: team size, communication links, and the importance of matching your process to your context rather than adopting a one-size-fits-all Agile playbook.
